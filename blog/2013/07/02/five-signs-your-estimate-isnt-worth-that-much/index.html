New problem domain

You probably don’t know enough of the inherent and accidental complexities of a problem domain of you never did a project in it.

So, you’re asked to build a new fancy global online payment service. How hard can it be, right? You can execute database transaction in your sleep, and know the basics of bookkeeping. Six to eight weeks for the MVP, right? Boom!

Turns out that most domain knowledge is not written down. It’s learned by hard experience.

Sorry, you don’t really know what the intrinsic complexity is of a new problem domain unless you did a few similar project in that field. Domain knowledge is not something you can learn from a book, it’s learned by experience. Until then: your estimate accuracy sucks.
